The history of all variables in a Round will be recorded in two history tables, one for each Game, like in the figure below. In the following table, you can see an example history of a Round in which the computer picked actions at random.

The recorded variables include:
  • Period -- Period number (6 is the current Period number in this example)
  • My Action -- Your action in every period of the current round for that game. In this example, in period 2 of
    Red Game
    your action is
    A
    .
  • Other Red's Action -- Action of Other Red in every period of the current round. In this example, in period 3, Other Red's action is
    B
    .
  • Other Blue's Action -- Action of Other Blue in every period of the current round. In this example, in period 4, Other Blue's action is
    Y
    .
  • My Points -- Your points in each period of the current round for that game.
  • Other Red's Points -- Points received by Other Red in each period of the current round in
    Red Game
    .
  • Other Blue's Points -- Points received by Other Blue in each period of the current round in
    Blue Game
    .
  • Number -- Random number generated by the computer at the end of the period to decide if the round continues for another period. A random number is drawn from {1,2,..., {{ dieN }}} with each number being equally likely.

There are ?s for "My Action", "Other Red's Action", "Other Blue's Action", "My Points", "Other Red's Points", and "Other Blue's Points" in Period 6 because that period is in progress in this example.
